About Us

Why Masters Athletics?

Masters Athletics, is a program for people 30+ to compete in various athletic events. Some of the many benefits include improved fitness, mental health, and the opportunity to achieve personal best times. An athlete competes against their own personal best standards.

The different events offered in Masters Athletics include track and field, cross country, and road running. Individuals are encouraged to join a local athletics club and get involved in the sport. Athletes need a valid ASA license to sign up with CGMA.

Chris Reed

Member Administrator

I have been running for over 60 years, with a few years off in my 50s, when I stopped because of injury, but took up mountainbiking. I was successful in my youth, winning numerous county championships and other races in the UK, and holding the UK Under 18 10,000m record with 31m30s. I didn't really fulfil my potential as a senior, but still recorded wins in a few road races, and fastest laps in road relays. I competed as a master in the UK, but the standard was so high, that I didn't make a lot of impact. On moving to South Africa in 2015, I resumed with Masters athletics, gaining a few age group podiums on road and track. In 2022, I developed bad osteoarthritis in both knees, and thought that was the end of my running, but with exercises, and an assortment of pills, it is keeping at bay. I really enjoy the friendly laid back atmosphere of Masters athletics, and hope I am able to continue for many years

Jackie Frey

Cross Country Representative

I started running when I was 52, bit of empty nest syndrome, I had 4 children and all very good at sport especially running, so I spent the best part of 17 years at the side of a track or Cross Country course. I started with road running and after a while I felt I needed something different, so I tried Cross Country, this where I met people from Gauteng Masters,I joined soon after and I have never looked back ! I have achieved various accolades including SA Masters Colours for track,Cross Country and Road running, but the most precious to me are the friends I have made along the way.

Azel de Villiers

Vice Chairperson, Track & Field Representative

I was overjoyed at discovering Masters Athletics in my forties! I have loved athletics since it was introduced to me in Grade 1, and from there I always competed in as many running and jumping events as possible at every meet. Since returning to Athletics, I decided to explore ‘throwing things’ and my debut in the Throws Pentathlon presented itself on a pure dare one day in August 2022, and I haven’t looked back since. The cherry on the cake at Masters Athletics is the friends you make. They have now become family. Athletics meets are my ‘active socials’ where we get together to run, jump and throw things! I am excited and honoured to be a committee member and I feel that part of my job will be to make every Athlete feel as part of the family as I do. If you need a motivator, encourager, friend, sister or assistant – I am here!

Bronwyn Roussot

Walking Representative
